About & Education

Dr. Deepender Kant Chhabra is a Principal Scientist and Senior Researcher at CSIR-Central Electronics Engineering Research Institute (CEERI), Pilani — one of India's oldest and most distinguished electronics research organisations under the Council of Scientific and Industrial Research.

His work sits at the intersection of electromagnetic theory and precision engineering, focused on the design, simulation, and development of vacuum electron devices (VEDs) — particularly klystrons — for applications spanning charged-particle accelerators, thermonuclear fusion plasma heating, high-power radar, and industrial microwave systems.

Over a career spanning decades at CSIR-CEERI, Dr. Chhabra has contributed to nationally significant projects including the development of an S-band klystron under an MoU between BARC and CEERI, and the design of a 100 kW CW power klystron for accelerator applications. He has mentored junior scientists and collaborative teams across India's leading scientific institutions.

His research employs advanced simulation tools — CST Particle Studio, HFSS, MAGIC, TRAK, Opera-3D, and ANSYS — to model electron guns, RF cavities, beam-wave interaction structures, and thermal systems with high fidelity before physical fabrication.

Research Areas

High Power Microwave Generation

Design and development of high-power microwave sources using vacuum electron devices, including multi-beam klystrons operating at S-band, L-band, and C-band frequencies.

Klystron Engineering

End-to-end klystron development — from electron gun design and RF cavity simulation to beam-wave interaction modeling, thermal analysis, and hot testing of multi-MW power devices.

Vacuum Electron Devices

Research into vacuum tubes and microwave amplifier tubes for radar, communication, and industrial applications — including multi-beam klystrons (MBKs) and extended-interaction structures.

Accelerator & Fusion Applications

Development of klystron amplifiers for charged-particle accelerators and plasma heating in thermonuclear fusion reactors — with direct national science and defence relevance.

Computational EM Simulation

Multi-physics simulation of RF and electron-beam systems using HFSS, CST Microwave Studio, CST Particle Studio, MAGIC, TRAK, Opera-3D, and ANSYS codes.

RF Cavity Design & Characterisation

Design and characterisation of re-entrant cavities, coaxial cavities, and multi-beam cavity structures — including novel tuning techniques for asymmetric multi-beam klystron geometries.

Notable Projects

CSIR-CEERI · Ongoing
100 kW CW Klystron for Particle Accelerators

Design and development of a 352.2 MHz, 100 kW continuous-wave power klystron for charged particle accelerator applications. Covers the complete design chain: electron gun, RF interaction cavities, collector thermal design, and multi-beam translation.

BARC – CEERI MoU
6 MW Peak Power S-Band Klystron

Development of a 6 MW peak, 24 kW average power S-band klystron under an official MoU between the Bhabha Atomic Research Centre (BARC) and CSIR-CEERI — one of India's most strategically significant vacuum tube development programmes.

CSIR-CEERI · Multi-beam Devices
Multi-Beam Klystron Research Programme

Research into multi-beam klystron (MBK) design methodology — developing scaling laws to translate single-beam parameters into multi-beam equivalents for S-band and C-band devices, reducing operating voltage while improving efficiency and power output.

CSIR-CEERI · RF Cavity Work
Novel Coaxial Cavity Tuning for MBK

Co-development of a new coaxial cavity design that suppresses asymmetric field distribution inherent in multi-beam klystron geometries, enabling stable single-post tuning and more homogeneous beam-wave interaction across all beam channels.
